# Byzantine Vernacular Greek Corpus

A compiled corpus of vernacular Byzantine Greek texts (12th-17th century) for computational linguistics, NLP, and frequency analysis. Designed to complement existing Ancient Greek corpora (GLAUx, Diorisis) and Modern Greek frequency data (FrequencyWords) by filling the gap for the vernacular/demotic register of Byzantine and early modern Greek.

## Corpus contents

The corpus currently contains **~191,000 words** across 12 texts (11 distinct works, with two editions of the Apokopos) spanning the 12th-17th centuries.

### Text descriptions

- **Digenes Akritas (Escorial MS)**: An excerpt from the Escorial manuscript of the Byzantine frontier epic about the half-Arab, half-Greek border warrior Digenes. The Escorial version is shorter and more archaic than the Grottaferrata manuscript.

- **Death of Digenes**: Two short folk ballads from the Digenes cycle, depicting the hero's death struggle (psychomachia) with Charos (Death personified).

- **Ptochoprodromika**: Satirical poems attributed to "Poor Prodromos," complaining about poverty, monastic life, and the indignity of a learned man's low status. Among the earliest vernacular Greek literary texts.

- **Chronicle of Moreas**: A verse chronicle of the Frankish conquest of the Peloponnese (1204-1292), written in 9,000+ political verses. The largest text in the corpus. Based on the Kalonaros 1940 edition.

- **Poulologos (Bird Parliament)**: A satirical beast poem in which birds gather and quarrel, mocking human social hierarchies.

- **Paidiophrastos Diegesis (Tale of the Quadrupeds)**: A beast fable in which animals hold court and debate, satirizing Byzantine society and its power structures.

- **Porikologos (Fruit Parliament)**: Two versions of a satirical text where fruits and vegetables debate and argue, paralleling the beast fable tradition. Included here are the Dimitrakos and St. Petersburg versions.

- **Peri Gerontos**: A comic poem advising old men against marrying young women, in the tradition of vernacular didactic satire.

- **Tale of the Donkey, Wolf, and Fox**: A beast fable in which the donkey outwits the wolf and the fox, with roots in Aesopic and oral traditions.

- **Apokopos**: A Cretan narrative poem by Bergadis describing a journey to the underworld. Two editions are included: a modern Wikisource transcription and Wagner's 1874 critical edition from *Carmina Graeca Medii Aevi* (Teubner).

- **Erotokritos**: The masterpiece of Cretan Renaissance literature, a 10,000-line verse romance by Vitsentzos Kornaros. Post-Byzantine in date but essential to the vernacular Greek literary tradition and the single largest text in the corpus.

## Notable gaps

The following vernacular Byzantine texts would strengthen this corpus but are not yet included. For each, the current availability of digital text or scans is noted.

### Texts with scans or partial digital availability

- **Digenes Akritas (Grottaferrata version)** - The most complete manuscript version of the epic, ~3,700 lines. The Mavrogordato critical edition (Oxford, 1956/1970) with parallel Greek text and English translation is freely available as a [PDF on Archive.org](https://archive.org/details/DigenesAkritesGreekEnglish). The Greek text could be extracted via OCR.

- **War of Troy** (Πόλεμος της Τρωάδος) - A 14,403-line verse paraphrase of Benoit de Sainte-Maure's *Roman de Troie*, composed in the 14th century in Frankish Peloponnese. The longest medieval Greek romance. Excerpts are available on [greek-language.gr](https://www.greek-language.gr/digitalResources/ancient_greek/anthology/mythology/browse.html?text_id=698) from both the Politis (1967) and Papathomopoulos-Jeffreys (1996) editions. The full critical edition (MIET, 1996) is in print only.

- **Erofili** (Ερωφίλη) - Cretan tragedy by Georgios Chortatsis (c. 1600), 3,205 verses. A page exists on [el.wikisource.org](https://el.wikisource.org/wiki/%CE%95%CF%81%CF%89%CF%86%CE%AF%CE%BB%CE%B7) but may be incomplete. The Bancroft-Marcus critical edition (Oxford, 2013) is in print. First printed in Venice in 1637.

- **Sacrifice of Abraham** (Η Θυσία του Αβραάμ) - Anonymous Cretan religious drama, ~1,144 verses. The Bakker-van Gemert critical edition (University of Crete Press, 1996) exists. Not found on Wikisource. Marshall's 1929 English translation (Oxford) of this and Erofili may be on Archive.org.

### Texts available only in print editions

- **Livistros and Rodamne** (Λίβιστρος και Ροδάμνη) - 13th c. romance, ~4,700 verses. Critical edition of the Vatican version by Lendari (MIET, 2007). English translation by Agapitos (Liverpool UP, 2021). No free digital text found.

- **Kallimachos and Chrysorroi** (Καλλίμαχος και Χρυσορρόη) - 14th c. romance attributed to Andronikos Palaeologos. Available in Betts' English translation (*Three Medieval Greek Romances*, Garland, 1996; Routledge reprint). No free Greek text found online.

- **Belthandros and Chrysantza** (Βέλθανδρος και Χρυσάντζα) - 13th-14th c. romance with strong connections to the acritic tradition. Available in Betts' English translation (same volume as above). No free Greek text found online.

- **Byzantine Achilleid** (Αχιλληΐδα) - 13th-14th c. romance loosely based on the Achilles legend. Survives in three manuscript versions (London, Naples, Oxford). No free digital Greek text found. The Smith edition (Vienna, 1999) is in print.

- **Imperios and Margarona** (Ιμπέριος και Μαργαρώνα) - 16th c. chivalric romance adapted from the French *Pierre de Provence et la belle Maguelonne*. Critical edition by Yiavis (MIET, 2019). No free digital text.

- **Florios and Platziaflora** (Φλώριος και Πλατζιαφλώρα) - Byzantine adaptation (c. 1400) of the French *Floire et Blanchefleur*. Only available in scholarly print editions.

- **Chronicle of the Tocco** (Χρονικόν των Τόκκων) - 15th c. Epirote chronicle in political verse, 3,923 surviving lines, covering the Tocco family's rise to power in Epirus (1375-1422). Critical edition by Schiro (Rome, 1975). No free digital text.

- **Spaneas** (Σπανέας) - 12th c. didactic poem in the "mirror for princes" tradition. No digital Greek text found on Wikisource or elsewhere.

### Restricted access

- **Cambridge Grammar of Medieval and Early Modern Greek corpus** - An electronic corpus of ~3 million words of vernacular Greek texts (11th-18th c.), compiled for the Cambridge Grammar project (Holton et al., Cambridge UP, 2019). The corpus is XML/TEI encoded but does not appear to be publicly available for download.

- **Thesaurus Linguae Graecae (TLG)** - Contains many of these texts but requires institutional subscription.

## Usage

### Fetch texts from sources

```bash
# Fetch Wikisource texts
python scripts/fetch_wikisource.py

# Fetch Chronicle of Moreas from kastra.eu
python scripts/fetch_chronicle_moreas.py
```

### Build combined corpus

```bash
# Build corpus.txt (all texts concatenated)
python scripts/build_corpus.py

# Build corpus with frequency analysis
python scripts/build_corpus.py --freq
```

This produces:
- `texts/corpus.txt` - combined corpus with text markers
- `texts/corpus_stats.json` - word counts per text and top-500 frequency list
- `texts/frequencies.tsv` - full word frequency list (with `--freq`)

### Export frequency JSON for dilemma

```bash
# Write byz_vern_freq.json to ../dilemma/data/
python scripts/export_freq_json.py --dilemma

# Or write to a custom path
python scripts/export_freq_json.py -o /path/to/output.json
```

The output format is compatible with [dilemma](https://github.com/ciscoriordan/dilemma)'s `rank_forms.py` frequency loader.

### Frequency output format

The `frequencies.tsv` file contains tab-separated word/count pairs, sorted by descending frequency:

```
word	count
και	5669
να	5590
το	4659
του	4382
...
```

## Directory structure

```
texts/              Raw text files and corpus outputs
  manifest.json     Metadata for all texts (source URLs, word counts, dates)
  corpus.txt        Combined corpus with text markers
  corpus_stats.json Word counts and frequency data
  frequencies.tsv   Full word frequency list
  *.txt             Individual text files
scripts/            Fetch and build scripts
  fetch_wikisource.py        Download texts from el.wikisource.org
  fetch_chronicle_moreas.py  Download Chronicle of Moreas from kastra.eu
  build_corpus.py            Combine texts into corpus with frequency analysis
  export_freq_json.py        Export frequencies as JSON for dilemma
```

## Notes on orthography

The texts use a mix of orthographic conventions:

- **Polytonic** (e.g., Apokopos, beast fables from Wagner editions): `ἐμᾶς`, `τὸν`, `ποὺ`
- **Monotonic** (e.g., Chronicle of Moreas from Kalonaros): `εμάς`, `τον`, `που`
- **Mixed** (e.g., Erotokritos): some sections use polytonic, others monotonic

For NLP purposes, normalizing to monotonic before processing is recommended.
